Self-love is difficult isn't it.

Written By Chaitanya June 20, 2021
Poetry

The mirror in my house is different you see,
The frame is painted a weird combination of colours - a dollop of self-pity mixed with
water that sparkles with lack of self-esteem,
The glass is speckled with imperfections,
Some days the speckles gather together and throng the mirror, so much so that
I can hardly see myself on it.

The pillows on my bed have been growing bigger from drinking tears ever since-
the first time a girl from school mocked at my teeth, the first time a boy told me
I had more hair on my forearms than a boy,
the first time a teacher called me
a shameful failure,
the first time I thought myself to be one,
And the insults grew snarkier by the day until they
tore out my flesh and seeped into my bones until there were no bones left anymore
just hollow emptiness clawing it's way into
veins waiting to be filled.
Self-love is difficult isn't it

When the air you breathe is tinted with judgements of your past
When you're told to make love to numbers when you're already in a relationship with words,
When you're told to be loud and talkative
but all you know is to remain meek and silent,
When being fat or skinny or just existing is another fucking mistake you made,
When everyone around you is a blinding bright star in the sky and you,
are just the small space of vacuum floating in the interlude,
When everyone else is a poem and you,
are just a single useless consonant letter,
It is terrifying
When you're supposed to look, laugh, live and love a certain way in order to be loved.
But how long will you remain single darling?
When will you swipe left to your insecurities
and swipe right to your confidence?
When will you go on a date with yourself?
When will you fall in love with
the marvellous wonder that you are?
